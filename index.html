Invited Speakers

Ira Kemlemacher-Schlizerman Professor of Computer Science at University of Washington and Principal Scientist at Google. Ira is a part of the GRAIL lab at UW and is a leading expert in the field of AR technology for online fashion and virtual try-on technology at Google

Michael Black is the founding director at the Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems, best known for his work on human motion and shape estimation; An entrepreneur (Body Labs spun out of his group was acquired by Amazon).

Gordon Wetzstein is an Associate Professor of Electrical Engineering and, by courtesy, of Computer Science at Stanford University. Gordon heads the Stanford Computational Imaging Lab, which produces many seminal and influential works in 2D-3D geometry understanding and modeling.

Christian Theobalt is the Director of Visual Computing department at Max Planck Institute for Informatics, and Professor at Saarland University. Christian is a renowned expert in 3D reconstruction and human modeling.

Sunil Hadap is a Principal Applied Scientist in Amazon’s Fashion & Fitness organization and co-leads the science for 3D Virtual Try-On and AR. He is credited for his simulation work on several block-buster Dreamworks movies including Shrek2, Madagascar and Kung Fu Panda.

Huamin Wang is the Chief Scientist at Style3D, where he leads Style3D’s global research team. His research focuses on physics-based animation, with a specific focus on high-performance and high-fidelity animation of digital human centered on virtual try-on technology.

Leonidas Guibas Professor and Head of the Geometric Computation group at Stanford, which produces many seminal and influential works in 3D geometry understanding and modeling.
